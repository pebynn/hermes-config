# R10-C Strategy Plan — strategy_chan_C.py

## Overview
Evolve R9-C (29.87% ann, 40.5% WR, 289 trades) toward R10-C by tightening entries, 
reducing stop-outs, raising profit targets, and improving trailing protection.

## Design Decisions

### 1. Entry Tightening: VOL_RATIO_MIN 2.5 → 3.5
**Rationale**: Higher volume threshold ensures strong institutional buying behind 
breakouts, reducing false signals that contributed to 172 losses.
**Alternative considered**: Adding separate MA20 slope confirmation — rejected 
because MA60 slope already provides trend direction, and additional moving average 
filter would compound the over-filtering risk (R8 lesson).
**Risk**: May reduce candidate pool. R9 had 289 trades; even 50% reduction yields 
~145 trades, still sufficient with MAX_HOLDINGS=10. If trade count drops below 50, 
this is flagged as failure mode.

### 2. Entry Quality: Bottom Fractal Confirmation
**Rationale**: Strengthen detect_fractal_bottom() by requiring close[i+1] > close[i] 
— the bar after the fractal low must close higher, confirming the reversal is real.
**Alternative considered**: Multi-bar volume confirmation — rejected as it would 
compound filtering without proven benefit in R9 data.
**Risk**: Slight reduction in buy2 signals (maybe 5-10%), but should improve 
signal quality proportionally more.

### 3. Stop-Loss: -6% → -5%
**Rationale**: R9 had 108 stop-losses (-8.4% avg). Tightening 1pp reduces false 
stop-outs while keeping risk manageable.
**Alternative considered**: Reviving ATR dynamic stop (ATR*1.5 as in R7) — 
rejected because R7 ATR had 164/186 triggers with only 3-4.5% effectiveness, 
and R8 explicitly disabled it. Fixed -5% is simpler and more predictable.
**Risk**: Narrower stop may whipsaw on volatile days. Mitigated by tighter 
trailing stop protecting profits before SL triggers.

### 4. Take-Profit: 40% → 50%
**Rationale**: Let top winners run further. R9's best trades hit 95.8% 
before TP truncation. Higher ceiling captures more upside.
**Risk**: Winners may peak below 50% and revert. Trailing stop at -6% 
provides partial protection.

### 5. Partial Take-Profit: 20% → 25%
**Rationale**: Aligns with the higher full TP target. Locks in more profit 
at half-position without waiting for full 50%.
**Risk**: Minimal — partial TP at +25% is still conservative.

### 6. Portfolio: MAX_HOLDINGS 8 → 10
**Rationale**: More diversification smooths returns and reduces single-stock risk. 
With stricter entry conditions, quality should remain high.
**Risk**: Dilution of best ideas. Mitigated by entry tightening ensuring only 
highest-quality candidates enter.

### 7. Trailing Stop: -8% → -6%
**Rationale**: Tighter trailing locks in profits sooner. R9's trailing stop 
60 trades averaged +1.56% with no overt cuts — tightening to -6% should not 
significantly reduce winners while providing better drawdown protection.
**Risk**: May cut some winners short. Given R9 trailing stop's positive track 
record (no cuts), -6% offers reasonable buffer.

### 8. Keepers (unchanged from R9-C / R8-C)
- LEVERAGE = 1.0 (iron rule)
- ATR_STOP_ENABLED = False (R8 decision, confirmed)
- MA20 > MA60 filter (R8-C keeper)
- MA60 slope > 0 (R6-C keeper)
- Equal weight 1/N (established convention)
- No golden cross (confirmed non-strategy)
- BUY2_ACTIVE_DAYS = 15 (R8-C, sufficient)
- REBALANCE_INTERVAL = 7 (weekly, standard)
- MIN_HOLDINGS = 6 (proportional to MAX_HOLDINGS=10)
- PARTIAL_TP_RATIO = 0.50 (standard half-position)

## Failure Modes
1. **R8-style over-filtering**: If VOL_RATIO 3.5 + fractal confirmation 
   cuts trade count from 289 to <50, this plan has failed. Fallback: revert 
   VOL_RATIO to 2.5 or 3.0.
2. **STOP_LOSS -5% whipsaw**: If stop-outs increase (not decrease), the 
   tighter stop is backfiring.
3. **Trailing stop -6% overtightening**: If average trailing stop trade 
   pnl drops below R9's +1.56%, adjustment needed.
