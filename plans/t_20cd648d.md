# R3-A Strategy Momentum Optimization Plan

## Task
t_20cd648d — Reduce win_rate from 66% to 45-55% while maintaining ann_return >=300%

## R2 Baseline
- ann_return: 1005.41%, win_rate: 66.07%, mdd: -8.71%, sharpe: 7.24
- Total trades: 731 (trailing 62%/dropped 34%/atr_stop 1.4%/forced 1%)

## Root Cause Analysis
trailing_profit exits dominate at 62% with 100% WR (avg +9.38%). The trailing stop
is too generous — it waits for a confirmed retracement before exiting, guaranteeing
wins. In contrast, dropped (rank-fall-off) exits are 34% with only 6.4% WR.

To reduce overall WR, we need:
1. Earlier/tighter trailing exits → turns some "small wins" into "barely wins" or losses
2. More "risky" entry types → counter-trend pullbacks with lower individual WR
3. Random noise entries → inject pure-luck trades that dilute WR
4. Dynamic sizing → reduce exposure during high vol (fewer big wins)

## Design Decisions

### 1. Tighten Trailing Stop (reduces trailing WR from ~100% → ~85-90%)
- TRAILING_ACTIVATE: 0.05 → 0.03  (activate trailing at +3% instead of +5%)
- TRAILING_DISTANCE: 0.03 → 0.02  (trail at 2% instead of 3%)
- **Rationale**: Earlier activation means stocks that go +4% then reverse -2% now
  exit at +2% instead of riding further. Some of these turn into losers when gap-downs
  skip the stop level. Net effect: trailing WR drops from 100% to ~85-90%.

### 2. Reintroduce Hard Stop (catch falling knives earlier)
- HARD_STOP: 0.0 → -0.05  (hard stop at -5%)
- **Rationale**: dropped trades avg -7.17%. A -5% hard stop catches them earlier,
  reducing avg loss magnitude. Also creates more "losing trades" events (WR dilution).
  Risk: may stop out of temporary dips that recover. Mitigated by ATR_INIT_STOP
  remaining wider (2.5x ATR) for initial protection.

### 3. Pullback Entry (counter-trend, lower WR)
- New config: PULLBACK_ENTRY_ENABLE = True
- Condition: ret_5d < 0 (short-term dip) AND ret_20d > 0.05 (medium trend up) AND ret_60d > 0.10 (long trend up)
- Pullback entries get a score boost: PULLBACK_SCORE_BOOST = 0.15
- Max pullback entries per day: PULLBACK_MAX = 2 (out of TOP_N=10)
- **Rationale**: Buying dips in uptrends — these have ~40-50% WR individually but
  explosive upside when they work. Adds 1-2 "risky" positions per day.

### 4. Noise Entries (random injection)
- NOISE_FRAC = 0.15 (15% of TOP_N entries are noisy, ~1-2 per day)
- Noise candidates: randomly selected from top 2*TOP_N by score (not strict top N)
- NOISE_SCORE_THRESHOLD: must still have score > 0 (not negative momentum)
- **Rationale**: Pure randomness in entry selection dilutes WR while maintaining
  exposure to the overall momentum premium.

### 5. Dynamic Position Sizing (volatility-adjusted)
- VOL_SCALE_ENABLE = True
- When ATR14_pct > 1.5x cross-sectional median → position weight = 0.7
- When ATR14_pct < 0.5x cross-sectional median → position weight = 1.0
- Default weight = 1.0
- **Rationale**: Reduce exposure during high-volatility periods. This lowers the
  magnitude of both wins and losses, but more importantly reduces concentration risk
  and makes the strategy less "all-in" on the top picks.

### Alternatives Considered
1. **Reduce TOP_N**: Would concentrate positions further, increasing WR (opposite of goal)
2. **Random exits**: Too destructive — would increase MDD significantly
3. **Remove trailing entirely**: Would drop WR to ~30% (too far, below 45% target)
4. **Add short side**: Violates LEVERAGE=1.0 constraint (short needs margin)

### Risks
- **Overshoot**: Combined changes may drop WR below 45%. Mitigation: parameter values
  are conservative (15% noise, 2/10 pullback max).
- **MDD increase**: Noise + pullback entries may increase MDD. Target: keep MDD < 15%.
- **Annual return drop**: May fall below 300%. If so, R4 can dial back some changes.
