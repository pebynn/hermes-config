### 1.4 Change 7 Detail: Signal-Z Dynamic Sizing

In `execute_entry`, after computing raw_weight from scores:
- Compute z-score of composite scores within signal batch
- For each signal:
  - z > 2.0: multiply weight by 2.0 (boost)
  - z < 0.5: multiply weight by 0.5 (reduce)
  - 0.5 <= z <= 2.0: weight unchanged
- Re-normalize after adjustment
- Keep 4% floor after adjustment

### 1.5 Alternative Approaches Considered

### 1.6 Risks

1. **Stop loss at -12% increases per-trade drawdown**: Higher risk per position, but R5 showed stop_loss triggered too early on winners. Mitigated by TP1 at 8% locking partial profit.
2. **MOM_20D_MIN=0.05 may reduce signal count too much**: Combined with sector tightening and candle confirm, could result in days with zero signals. Acceptable — quality over quantity.
3. **TP1 at 8% half-sell may cap winners**: Strong stocks that would have run to 18% get trimmed at 8%. But backtest shows take_profit_half was already at 15% in R5 — lowering to 8% actually locks earlier but leaves more time for remainder to reach 18%.
4. **Signal-Z dynamic sizing may over-concentrate**: If one stock has z>>2.0, it gets 2x weight. With MAX_POSITIONS=6, worst case is ~33% allocation. Acceptable with position cap of 300k.

### 2.4 Signal-Z Dynamic Sizing in execute_entry (around line 550-555)

After computing raw_weight:
```python
if DYNAMIC_SIZING and "score" in signals.columns and len(signals) >= 3:
    # Compute z-score of scores within this batch
    score_z = (signals["score"] - signals["score"].mean()) / max(signals["score"].std(), 1e-8)
    # Boost: z>2.0 → 2x; Reduce: z<0.5 → 0.5x
    adj = pd.Series(1.0, index=signals.index)
    adj[score_z > 2.0] = 2.0
    adj[score_z < 0.5] = 0.5
    raw_weight = raw_weight * adj.values
    raw_weight = raw_weight / raw_weight.sum()  # re-normalize
    raw_weight = raw_weight.clip(lower=0.04)     # 4% floor
```
