## 1. Problem Statement

R6-B over-filtered: 77 entry signals in 242 trading days across 5331 stocks (93% signal reduction
vs R5's 1188). Annual return dropped from +75.62% (R5) to +13.84% (R6). The core tension:
R5 was too loose (515 stop-loss exits, 43% exit rate), R6 was too tight (nearly zero stops, but
barely any entries). R7 must find the midpoint — wider aperture than R6 but with quality gates
preserved.

Four filter layers compound to eliminate >93% of R5 signals:
  CANDLE_CONFIRM (ret_1d_lag>0) → MOM_20D=5% → SECTOR_HARD=0.65 → MAX_POSITIONS=6

Each independently reduces candidates; combined they form an impossible funnel.
38% of R6's few exits were rebalance_close_full (avg -3501 loss) — 3-day holding window too short.

## 2. Design Decisions

### Change 1: Relax CANDLE_CONFIRM (ret_1d_lag > 0 → ret_1d_lag > -0.01)
- **Rationale**: R6's `ret_1d_lag > 0` eliminates ~60% of candidates. A reversal play doesn't
  need yesterday's candle to close positive — it needs a DECLINE to reverse FROM. Allowing
  -0.01 (essentially flat) captures stocks that bottomed intraday and closed near the low.
  The 5-day decline prefiler (ret_5d < -3%) already ensures these are declining stocks.
- **Alternative considered**: Complete removal of CANDLE_CONFIRM. Rejected — would lose the
  reversal-day quality signal entirely. threshold=-0.02 also considered but -0.01 is
  conservative enough.
- **Risk**: May admit stocks still in free-fall (ret_1d_lag=-0.01 is very close to flat).
  Mitigation: momentum_pass (>2%) still requires medium-term uptrend. A stock down 5% in
  a week but up 2% over 20 days with ret_1d_lag=-0.01 is likely bottoming.
- **Code locations**:
  - Line 74: `CANDLE_CONFIRM_THRESHOLD = -0.01` (new constant, was implicit >0)
  - Line 434: `candidates[ret_1d_lag] > -0.01` (was `> 0`)
  - Test: test_candle_confirm_relaxed (ret_1d_lag=-0.005 passes, -0.015 fails)

### Change 2: Lower MOM_20D_MIN (0.05 → 0.02)
- **Rationale**: 5% momentum is a strong bar for A-shares — most don't sustain 5% 20-day.
  2% captures early reversal stocks that have just started turning. The OR condition
  (close > MA60) still provides an independent quality gate.
- **Alternative**: Use 3-day momentum. Rejected: 20-day provides noise filtering.
- **Risk**: May pick up stocks with continuing decline masked by noise. Mitigation:
  momentum_pass has the `close > MA60` OR gate as secondary check.
- **Code locations**: Line 107: `MOM_20D_MIN = 0.02`
- **Test**: test_mom_threshold_relaxed (mom=0.03 passes, mom=-0.01 only passes if close>MA60)

### Change 3: Relax SECTOR_HEAT_HARD_FILTER (0.65 → 0.50)
- **Rationale**: 0.65 = sector in top 35% — this cut most candidates in R6. 0.50 restores
  the R5 baseline (~50% of sectors pass). Sector_boost still multiplies into score, so
  weaker sectors get lower allocation weight.
- **Alternative**: Remove sector hard filter entirely. Rejected: sector quality matters.
- **Risk**: Weaker sector environments. Mitigation: sector_boost in score means lower-ranked
  stocks from weak sectors take smaller positions.
- **Code locations**: Line 83: `SECTOR_HEAT_HARD_FILTER = 0.50`
- **Test**: test_sector_filter_relaxed (0.55 passes, 0.45 fails)

### Change 4: Increase MAX_POSITIONS (6 → 10)
- **Rationale**: 6 positions forces concentration; 10 spreads risk across more reversal bets.
  Also allows weaker-but-still-positive signals through the funnel.
- **Risk**: Dilution per position. Mitigation: dynamic sizing ensures best signals get
  proportional capital allocation.
- **Code locations**: Line 63: `MAX_POSITIONS = 10`
- **Test**: test_max_positions_increased

### Change 5: Lower VOL_RATIO_MIN (1.2 → 1.0) [OPTIONAL — IMPLEMENTING]
- **Rationale**: 1.2 = 20% above-average volume (= strong capitulation). 1.0 allows
  normal-volume reversals, which are often quieter but equally valid. Volume still
  enters composite z-score, so higher-volume stocks rank higher.
- **Risk**: Lower conviction entries. Mitigation: volume weight in composite score (0.3)
  preserves volume-quality signal.
- **Code locations**: Line 86: `VOL_RATIO_MIN = 1.0`
- **Test**: test_vol_ratio_relaxed

### Change 6: Increase REBALANCE_DAYS (3 → 5) [OPTIONAL — IMPLEMENTING]
- **Rationale**: R6's 3-day window killed 38% of positions via rebalance_close_full
  (avg loss -3501). 5 days gives winners time to develop and reduces forced exits.
  This is the primary lever for reducing the 38% exit rate.
- **Alternative**: Disable rebalance entirely. Rejected: periodic rebalancing is needed
  to rotate into better signals.
- **Risk**: May hold losers longer than 3 days. Mitigation: stop loss (-12%) and
  conditional time_stop (5d, only if ret < -5%) still provide safety exits.
- **Code locations**: Line 91: `REBALANCE_DAYS = 5`
- **Test**: test_rebalance_days_increased

### Change 7: Extreme Signal Regression [OPTIONAL — IMPLEMENTING]
- **Rationale**: When a candidate's composite score is above the 95th percentile of all
  candidates on a given day, the signal quality is high enough to skip CANDLE_CONFIRM.
  This is the "strong conviction exception" — if everything else is screaming BUY, the
  candle direction shouldn't stop it.
- **Alternative**: Lower CANDLE_CONFIRM threshold further (e.g., -0.02). Rejected: would
  degrade quality for ALL entries, not just the strongest ones.
- **Mechanism**: In select_signals, compute score BEFORE candle filter → identify
  candidates with score > 95th percentile → pass them through regardless of candle.
- **Risk**: Top-scoring stocks may have bearish candles. Mitigation: score already
  includes sector momentum (0.65+ now 0.50+), decline extremity, RSI oversold, and
  volume capitulation — composite signal quality is high regardless of candle.
- **Code locations**: select_signals(), between Steps 3 and 4. New constant:
  `EXTREME_SIGNAL_QUANTILE = 0.95` and `EXTREME_SIGNAL_BYPASS = True`
- **Test**: test_extreme_signal_bypasses_candle (candidate with score in top 5%
  passes even with negative ret_1d_lag)
